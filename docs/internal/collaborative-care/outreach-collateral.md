# Oasis Health Services: Provider Outreach Collateral & Strategy Guide

This guide outlines the recommended marketing and informational collateral for introducing the Collaborative Care Model (CoCM) partnership program to primary care practices (PCPs). It includes structural recommendations, copy templates for physical printouts, and best practices for in-person provider visits.

---

## 1. Collateral Strategy Matrix

When visiting busy clinics, you will encounter different roles with distinct concerns. Your collateral should be tailored to match:

| Collateral Type | Target Audience | Key Objective | Ideal Format | Scan Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-Page Executive Flyer** | Practice Managers & Lead Physicians | Hook interest, highlight operational simplicity and billing feasibility. | Single-sided premium cardstock. | **30 seconds** |
| **Billing Reference Card** | Billing & Coding Managers | Remove the fear of complex time-based billing; provide immediate, actionable CPT codes. | Laminated index card or half-sheet flyer. | **15 seconds** |
| **CoCM Clinical Brochure** | Clinic Nurses & Care Managers | Explain the workflow, "warm handoff," and the team feedback loop. | Tri-fold brochure (featuring the flow diagram). | **1 minute** |
| **Partnership Presentation Deck** | Board/Owners & Decision Makers | Formal contract review, PSA details, revenue share agreements, and registry setup. | Digital PDF or printed booklet for follow-up meetings. | **10-15 minutes** |

### **[HEADER] Integrated Mental Health Care, Simplified.**
*Partner with Oasis Health Services to deliver evidence-based Collaborative Care (CoCM) directly within your primary care clinic.*

### **Why Collaborative Care?**
Primary care is the new frontline for mental health. Managing psychiatric patient needs without in-house specialists leads to referral delays, fragmented treatment, and clinician burnout. 
The **Collaborative Care Model (CoCM)** is a clinically proven, Medicare-approved framework that integrates behavioral health directly into your clinic.

### **The Oasis Turn-Key Solution**
We remove the operational friction of setting up an integrated practice. We offer two flexible models depending on your clinic's resources:
1.  **Oasis-Provided Care Manager (Shared Staffing):** We employ and embed a qualified Behavioral Health Care Manager into your practice's workflow (virtually or physically). We supply the Psychiatric Consultant and the software registry, requiring zero hiring overhead from you.
2.  **PCP-Employed Care Manager (Consultant-Only):** If you already have care management staff, they manage the patient registry while Oasis provides the remote Psychiatric Consultant for weekly caseload reviews and diagnostic support.

### **Key Benefits for Your Practice:**
*   **Mitigate Provider Burnout:** Delegate time-intensive symptom monitoring, outcome tracking, and brief counseling to a dedicated Care Manager.
*   **Clinically Proven Outcomes:** Backed by over 90 randomized controlled trials, CoCM achieves a 50% or greater reduction in anxiety and depression symptoms compared to traditional external referrals.
*   **Sustainable Practice Revenue:** CoCM is fully reimbursable under standard medical benefits. Your clinic bills for the cumulative monthly time spent by the Care Manager using Medicare-approved time-based CPT codes (99492, 99493, G2214).

## 3. Template: Billing & Coding Pocket Guide

*Recommendation: Print this on a double-sided, pocket-sized laminated card. Leave this with the Practice Manager or Billing Specialist.*

### **[SIDE A: CoCM CPT Code Reference]**

| CPT Code | Type | Description | Minimum Time (Midpoint) | Billing Range (per calendar month) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **99492** | Initial Month | First month of care coordinator tracking. | **36 mins** | 36–85 minutes / month |
| **99493** | Subsequent Months | Ongoing monthly care management. | **31 mins** | 31–75 minutes / month |
| **G2214** | Short-Unit | Low-intensity or transitional months. | **16 mins** | 16–30 minutes / month |
| **99494** | Add-on | Billed in conjunction with 99492/99493. | **16 mins** | Each additional 30 minutes |

### **[SIDE B: Quick Compliance Checklist]**
*   [ ] **Initiating Visit:** Ensure the patient has had a face-to-face E/M, Annual Wellness, or TCM visit within the last 12 months before starting CoCM.
*   [ ] **Patient Consent:** Document verbal or written patient consent in the EHR, indicating they are aware that standard medical coinsurance/copays apply.
*   [ ] **Time-Tracking:** Log all cumulative minutes spent by the Care Manager (patient calls, registry management, psychiatrist consultation) within the calendar month. Only care manager activities count toward the code time.
*   [ ] **Weekly Reviews:** Confirm the Care Manager and Oasis Psychiatric Consultant conduct weekly caseload reviews for active registry patients.

## 4. Best Practices for In-Person Clinic Visits

1.  **Avoid Clinic Rush Hours:** Drop in between **10:00 AM and 11:30 AM** or **2:00 PM and 3:30 PM**. Avoid early mornings, lunch hours, or the end-of-day rush.
2.  **Target the Gatekeeper (Practice Manager):** Physicians are rarely available for unscheduled drop-ins. Introduce yourself to the front desk and ask to speak briefly with the **Practice Manager** or **Clinical Lead**.
3.  **The 30-Second Elevator Pitch:**
    > *"Hi, I'm [Name] from Oasis Health Services. We partner with primary care clinics to help them launch the Collaborative Care Model (CoCM)—providing them with remote psychiatric consultants and embedded care managers. It's fully reimbursable under standard medical benefits, relieves PCP burnout, and integrates behavioral health directly into your EHR. I wanted to leave this 1-page overview and our billing pocket guide with you."*
4.  **Secure the Follow-Up:** Don't try to close a contract on a drop-in. Ask: *"Who is the best person to email to schedule a brief 10-minute Zoom call to discuss how this model fits your clinic?"*
5.  **Leave the Collateral:** Always leave a neat folder containing the **1-Page Executive Flyer**, the **Clinical Flow Brochure**, and the **Laminated Billing Reference Card**.
